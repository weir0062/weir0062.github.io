Artemis IV mission to the moon in VR

In my last year of GameDev, i was lucky enough to be on the team of students chosen to do a virtual reality version of the Arthemis IV space launch. Grapple Heroes, a multiplayer race with grapple guns

After my last year of GameDev, me and a few fellow graduates worked on a multiplayer race game, in which you had to get to the objective first, while avoiding all of the obstacles in your way. In order to get to the objective, you will need to grapple to special points, using either the grappling towards, or the swing functionality. The combination of the two make for a very entertaining, yet challenging competition. I was one of the three programmers on the team and my responsibilities included:
-Designed and Developed initial Game Design
-Integrated all of the grappling functionality( Swing/Pull towards/Crosshair)
-Integrated all of the animations
-Integrated all of the camera functionality
-Created Shader for non playable areas
-Developed some of the interactions

Stealth level

This stealth game project was an opportunity for me to sharpen my programming skills and showcase my expertise in game development. I designed and implemented advanced AI path-following algorithms, which included basic save and load functionality, seamless animation transitions, and smooth third-person camera movements. One of the unique features of the game is the "danger zone" mechanic, which I coded entirely in shader language for improved performance and graphical fidelity. By utilizing my proficiency in both C# and shader programming, I was able to create a proof-of-concept that highlights my technical abilities and attention to detail.
