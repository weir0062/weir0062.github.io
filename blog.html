Artemis IV mission to the moon in VR

In my last year of GameDev, i was lucky enough to be on the team of students chosen to do a virtual reality version of the Arthemis IV space launch. We worked on this project for 8 months, with on-site work at the museum multiple times a week. I was one of the three programmers on the team and my responsibilities included:
-Designed and programmed main menu.
-Designed and programmed in-game tablet menu.
-Developed gravity controller.
-Designed and implemented fader.
-Developed sound on collision system.
-Added Haptic Feedback to controllers.
-Resolved UI issues and implemented improvements for optimal UX.

Stealth level

This stealth game project was an opportunity for me to sharpen my programming skills and showcase my expertise in game development. I designed and implemented advanced AI path-following algorithms, which included basic save and load functionality, seamless animation transitions, and smooth third-person camera movements. One of the unique features of the game is the "danger zone" mechanic, which I coded entirely in shader language for improved performance and graphical fidelity. By utilizing my proficiency in both C# and shader programming, I was able to create a proof-of-concept that highlights my technical abilities and attention to detail.
